The Design

Simplicity that
speaks volumes

Mid-Century refers to works from the 1950s and 60s, characterized by the use of materials like wood, metal, cane and fabrics. The beauty of it lies in its simplistic and organic flowing lines that come together to create a form that serves a specific purpose. The Barstool distils this language into a single, purposeful gesture—an elevated seat that belongs wherever people gather.

An exploration of the essence behind the art and craft spread across this diverse country and beyond.

— The Studio Philosophy

Craftsmanship

Every detail,
considered

01
Honest Joinery

Traditional mortise and tenon joints crafted in solid teak and honne. No veneer, no shortcuts—just wood meeting wood with precision that lasts generations.

02
Woven Comfort

Hand-selected fabric and leather upholstery that ages gracefully. Each piece develops its own character over time, growing warmer and richer with daily use.

03
Proportioned Form

At 93cm, the barstool is calibrated for standard counter heights. The 51.5cm width provides comfortable seating without crowding—geometry in service of living.

Material Palette

Letting materials
speak for themselves

The Barstool is built from sustainably sourced hardwoods—teak, honne, and matti—chosen for their warmth, durability, and honest grain. Leather and fabric upholstery is selected for how it ages: not against time, but with it.
