The Noku Feel

Cinematic. Gallery-like.
Warm minimalism.

The Noku design language is cinematic, gallery-like, and contemplative. It is warm minimalism—not cold or sterile. Products lead, text supports. Every surface tells a story through generous space and confident restraint. Premium is defined by what you exclude, not what you include.

Product-First

70% Visual, 30% Text

Images dominate. Photography emphasizes texture, grain, weave, and craft. Every image invites the viewer to reach out and touch the material.

Quiet Luxury

Whisper, Never Shout

Subtle motion—hover effects, scroll reveals—all gentle, never flashy. Confident negative space communicates luxury. Each page tells a narrative.

We exist to craft the future of Indian furniture — blending traditional craftsmanship and modern design into pieces that belong in your home.

Typography

Two typefaces that bridge
tradition and modernity.

Josefin Sans brings geometric, modern confidence to headings. Quattrocento adds elegant serif warmth to body text. Together they embody Noku's blend of innovation and heritage.

Spacing & Grid

Whitespace is sacred.
Every pixel is intentional.

Every element aligns to an 8px grid. Whitespace communicates confidence and luxury. It is not empty—it is breathing room. Never use arbitrary spacing like 23px, 37px, or 45px.

Layout Patterns

Derived from the
Noku websites.

These layout patterns come directly from noku.framer.website and nokustudio.webflow.io. Apply them consistently across all mediums.

Split Layout

50/50 or 60/40. Image bleeds to edge, text side has 48-64px padding. Used for collections and features.

Vertical Storytelling

Alternating image-text blocks. Narrative flow where each section builds on the previous. Timeline feel.

Product Grid

3-4 columns, no borders or shadows. Image fills container, name below. Hover: subtle 1.02 scale.

Components

No borders.
No shadows.
Only space.

Whitespace is the primary separator. Cards are borderless. Hover states are subtle. Dividers are 1px Chamoisee, used sparingly.

Component Rules

Cards

No borders, no shadows. Image fills top, text below with padding.

Buttons

Gunmetal bg, Bone text, 4-8px radius. Hover: opacity or Chamoisee shift.

Links

Chamoisee color, no underline default, underline on hover.

Hover States

Subtle scale (1.02), opacity, or color shift. Never jarring.

Footer

4-column grid. White/light background, NOT dark. 64-80px padding.

Voice & Tone

The design whispers
rather than shouts.

Approachable yet sophisticated. Warm yet professional. Active voice, short sentences, no jargon. Focus on craftsmanship and quality.

✓ Use This Approach

"Designed with intent. Crafted with care."

"Pieces that belong in your home"

"Where tradition meets tomorrow"

"Quality that lasts generations"

Active voice. Short sentences. No jargon. Focus on craft.

✗ Avoid This Approach

"Exceptional premium at unbeatable prices!"

"Buy now and transform instantly"

"Exclusive limited-time offer"

"Don't miss out!"

Marketing clichés. Pressure tactics. Urgency. Jargon.

To be the go-to creative studio that gives form to the treasured furniture that you want, where every step of your journey unfolds into an exceptional and unforgettable experience.

Noku Studio Vision

Premium Design Checklist

Before finalizing
any design.

Verify every item below. This checklist ensures the output meets Noku's premium, designer-made standard.

Brand Compliance

Colors: Only the four brand colors
Typography: Josefin Sans headings, Quattrocento body
Logo: Chamoisee SVG fill, properly placed
Voice: Warm, approachable, sophisticated
Materials: Highlights wood, leather, cane, fabric, brass

Spacing & Layout

Whitespace: Min 24px margins, generous breathing room
Grid: All spacing uses 8px multiples
Sections: 80-120px padding, alternating light/dark
Text width: 600-700px max for paragraphs
Content density: 60-65% max, never cramped

Premium Feel

Hierarchy: Immediately clear what's most important
Restraint: No decoration—all elements serve a purpose
Photography: High-res, clean, material-forward
Consistency: Similar elements look identical
Luxury standard: Would a premium brand be proud?
